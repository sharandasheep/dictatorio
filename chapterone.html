Chapter One: What Makes A Good Dictator

Adolf Hitler of Nazi Germany

Adolf Hitler was one of the most influential leaders in all of history, up there with Napoleon, Lincoln, and many other leaders that did great things, even if those things were terrible.

Hitler was born in Austria and was raised by a mother he loved and a father he hated. In Hitler’s childhood, his father was a civil servant and died in 1903. Hitler dropped out of secondary school and wanted to pursue his dream of becoming an artist. He was rejected from Vienna’s fine arts academy. After his mother died in 1908, he moved to Vienna and started selling images. He read a lot of politics and this led to ideas of Nazi. Hitler had some service in Munich in WW1. One notable thing was that he earned a medal called the Iron Cross for his bravery in WWI and he wore it until he died. This shows how much he values this bravery and being the best. After Hitler returned to Munich in 1918, he joined the small German Worker’s party. This was a strong German nationalist group. He was skilled in speech and propaganda and was very persuading. Hitler used the swastika symbol of peace that would later be a representation of terror and and power. Hitler led the growing Nazi party which punished the terms of the Versailles Treaty. He recruited Ernest Rohm who got the strong arm squads (Sturmabteilung-SA). On November 8, 1923, members of the SA marched into a beer hall. Led by Hitler, they proclaimed the beginning of a national revolution. They got into a gun fight with the police and fled, but were later captured and sent to jail. Hitler was obsessed with ethnic purity. He placed the Aryan Race on top. He also established the Hitler youth for kids to also join and the Schutzstaffel (SS), a better alternative to SA. In 1932, Hitler ran against Hindenburg for president and was declared chancellor. The government passed a law that the Nazi Party constitutes as the only political party in Germany. When Hindenburg died, Hitler was declared commander of all armed forces. His power continued to grow, and his officer Goebbels of the Propaganda Department helped him win the hearts of the people. In his time as dictator, he made the Holocaust and started WWII, invading some of the strongest countries at the time, such as Poland and France.

Joseph Stalin of Soviet Russia

While Hitler got all the attention for starting WWII and the Holocaust and also for his master race, Stalin was the most evil dictator of them all. Killing not just people of other counries that were enemies during the war but also setting up concentration camps called the Gulag system that killed his own people.

In his childhood, Stalin was bullied and never taken seriously, so he grew with a quest for vengeance to earn power. He was born in modern-day Georgia which at the time was a part of Russia. He was exiled to Siberia once for organizing a labor strike. He gained power and became general secretary of the Communist Party. He assisted Vladimir Lenin in fleeing to Finland in the revolution, with the help of Travski. When Lenin died, Stalin seized control of Russia and did many things. He signed a pact with Hitler and Nazi Germany which would end up being broken, forcing Russia into WWII. He killed many of his own people in the attempt to boost the industrial state. He took over the peasant farms and made them state-control, starting a famine in Russia. Overall, Stalin did many things.
